Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common questions about traveling to Kurdistan

Is Kurdistan safe for tourists?

Yes! The Kurdistan Region is known for its safety and stability. It has its own security forces and has been a safe haven for tourists for many years. Millions of visitors travel to Kurdistan annually without incident.

Do I need a visa to visit Kurdistan?

Citizens of many countries can obtain a visa on arrival at Erbil and Sulaymaniyah international airports. The visa is typically valid for 30 days. Check with your local Kurdistan representation for specific requirements.

What is the best time to visit Kurdistan?

Spring (March-May) and autumn (September-November) are ideal, with pleasant temperatures and beautiful landscapes. Summer is hot in the plains but comfortable in the mountains. Winter brings snow to higher elevations.

What currency is used in Kurdistan?

The Iraqi Dinar (IQD) is the official currency, but US Dollars are widely accepted. ATMs are available in major cities, and credit cards are accepted at hotels and larger establishments.

What languages are spoken?

Kurdish (Sorani and Kurmanji dialects) is the primary language. Arabic is also official. English is increasingly spoken, especially in tourist areas, hotels, and restaurants.

How do I get around Kurdistan?

Car rentals are available and recommended for flexibility. Taxis and ride-sharing services operate in cities. For visiting multiple destinations, hiring a driver or joining organized tours is popular and convenient.
